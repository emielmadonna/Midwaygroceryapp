Getting found online for RV & tent camping.

A plain-English plan to rank near Leavenworth & Lake Wenatchee — what to do, in what order, and why it works.

PREPARED JUNE 2026  ·  midwayplain.com

The one thing to understand first

When someone searches “RV park near Leavenworth” or “camping near Lake Wenatchee,” Google shows a little map with 3 pins at the top. That map is where most campers click — and it is not controlled by your website.

It’s controlled by your Google Business Profile, your reviews, and being listed on the right camping sites. So the biggest wins are things you do off the website. Your website (now improved) wins the normal results underneath the map and turns visitors into bookings.

Translation: do the Google Business Profile and reviews first. That’s where the ranking lives.

If you only do five things

In order. Each one builds on the last.

  1. Set up your Google Business Profile — add “Campground” & “RV park,” photos, hours.
  2. Start collecting Google reviews — a QR code by the register. This is the #1 ranking booster.
  3. Get listed on the camping sites — The Dyrt, Campendium, Hipcamp, Good Sam.
  4. Pitch a story to the Echo — a local news link is gold (ready-to-send email inside).
  5. Turn on visitor tracking — so we can see what’s working. (We’ll set this up for you.)
1

Your Google Business Profile

This is the free listing that shows your pin, hours, photos and reviews on Google and Google Maps. It is the single most important thing for getting found. Set it up at business.google.com.

Because the camping operates under the “Midway” name (no separate sign), you use one listing with extra categories added — not a second listing. A duplicate listing can get your business suspended.

  • Claim & verify the listing if you haven’t already.
  • Add the camping categories. Keep your main category as the store/gas station, then add “Campground” and “RV park” as extra categories. This is what makes you show up for camping searches.
  • Upload 10+ real photos — RV sites with hookups, tent spots, the fire pits, the store, the view. Add a few new ones each month. Fresh photos help.
  • Set your hours and note that fuel is 24/7. Google uses these hours in search, so keep them current here.
  • Fill the description with “Plain, WA,” “near Leavenworth,” “Lake Wenatchee,” “Chiwawa Loop Road.”
  • Turn on attributes — pets allowed, Wi-Fi, RV hookups, restrooms.
  • Post seasonally — “RV & tent sites open for summer.” Free, and it helps.
2

Reviews — your secret weapon biggest lever

More recent, positive Google reviews means a higher spot on the map and more clicks. Even 15–25 reviews will beat an empty competitor. This is the highest-payoff habit you can build.

  • Get your review link from your Business Profile (“Ask for reviews”).
  • Print a small QR-code card for the register and the campsite check-in: “Camped with us? Leave a quick review →”
  • Just ask. Every happy camper at checkout. The asking is what works.
  • Reply to every review — good and bad. Google rewards it and it looks great to future guests.
  • Collect reviews on camping sites too (next page) — The Dyrt, Campendium, Hipcamp.
3

Get listed where campers look

4

The Echo — your local-news angle

A link from your local paper is one of the strongest signals you can get, and competitors can’t copy it. The trick: pitch a story, not an ad — and ask that the online version link to midwayplain.com. Send this from your own email:

✉   To: The Echo  ·  Subject: Story idea — Midway adds RV & tent camping on Chiwawa Loop Road

Hi [Editor name],

I’m [your name] from Midway Gas & Grocery out on Chiwawa Loop Road in Plain. Alongside the store and 24/7 fuel, we’ve opened up full-hookup RV sites and tent camping for the summer — a little basecamp for folks heading to Leavenworth and Lake Wenatchee.

I thought it might make a nice local piece — a working country store turning into a place to stay the night, what we’re seeing from travelers this season, and what’s new for locals. Happy to host a photographer; the sites and the valley look great this time of year.

If it’s a fit, could the online version link back to our site (midwayplain.com) so readers can find us? Thanks either way —

[Name] · Midway Gas & Grocery · (509) 596-1076 · midwayplain.com

Do this seasonally — opening weekend, a fall-colors piece, an event around the fire. Each story is another local link. Also ask if the Echo has a free business directory and get listed there too.

5

Keep it up — about 30 minutes a month

SEO isn’t one-and-done. A little upkeep compounds. Once a month:

  • Add a few fresh photos to your Google Business Profile and post a seasonal update.
  • Ask that week’s campers for reviews, and reply to any new ones.
  • Send one local link request per season — the Echo, a Leavenworth tourism site, a neighbor business.
  • Glance at your visitor tracking (once we set it up) to see which pages campers land on.
